The WireSocket infrastructure is divided into two distinct layers: the **Control Plane** and the **Data Plane**.

While the Data Plane handles the real-time synchronization of your Yjs documents, the **Control Plane** is responsible for the management, security, and regional orchestration of your ecosystem.

---

## Core Elements

The Control Plane manages four primary entities:

### 1. Tenant

A **Tenant** represents your organization or root account.

- Top-level container for resources, billing, and team members.
- Each Tenant has its own unique **Signing Keys** for JWT issuance, ensuring cryptographic isolation from other organizations.

### 2. Apps

Within a Tenant, you can create multiple **Apps**. Each App represents a specific product or environment.

- **Identity**: Each App holds its own unique `client_id` and `client_secret`.
- **Data Residency**: Every App is pinned to a **License Region** (e.g., `aws-us-east-1`) during creation.
- **Database isolation**: Separate from the real-time sync, each App is assigned a specific internal database instance (`DbCode`), ensuring your document metadata is geographically localized and isolated within Turso shards.

### 3. Plans

A **Plan** defines the performance characteristics and usage limits for an App.

- **Limits**: Controls maximum concurrent connections, document counts, and operations per minute (OPM).
- **The "One Free" Rule**: Each Tenant is limited to **exactly one application on a Free Plan**. Additional applications require a paid subscription managed through Dodo Payments.
- **Flexibility**: Plans can be upgraded or downgraded through the dashboard. Paid-to-paid transitions are prorated immediately.

### 4. Nodes (Infrastructure Registry)

The Control Plane acts as the **Global Registry** for the Data Plane's physical infrastructure.

- **Node Management**: It maintains the registry of all active and inactive WebSocket nodes across the globe (e.g., `eu-central-1.wiresocket.net`).
- **Regional Orchestration**: It exposes APIs to activate or deactivate nodes, enabling zero-downtime maintenance and fleet scaling.
- **Discovery Backbone**: The Control Plane powers the [Discovery process](/data-plane/dataplane#document-discovery), allowing clients to resolve the correct physical node for any document.

## Security Enforcement

The Control Plane is where you define the security boundaries that the Data Plane then enforces at the edge.

### Allowed Domains

To prevent **Token Theft**, you can whitelist up to 3 domains (e.g., `myapp.com`, `localhost:3000`).

- **Baked-in security**: These domains are embedded directly into your JWT claims.
- **Edge validation**: Data Plane nodes check the `Origin` header of every incoming connection against these claims. If they don't match, the connection is rejected.

---

## The Token Lifecycle

WireSocket uses a standard **OAuth 2 Machine-to-Machine (M2M)** flow. Your backend server exchanges its App credentials for a token, which is then passed to your frontend clients.

### Security First: Refresh Tokens

WireSocket supports **Refresh Tokens** out of the box to minimize the exposure of your sensitive credentials.

1. **Access Token**: Short-lived (15 minutes). Used by your clients to connect to the Data Plane.
2. **Refresh Token**: Long-lived. Used by your backend to request new Access Tokens without needing to re-send the `client_secret`.

<Info>
  **Best Practice**: Store your `client_secret` in a secure vault (like Doppler
  or AWS Secrets Manager). Your auth service should only use the secret once
  during a "cold start" to get a token pair, then use the Refresh Token for all
  subsequent rotations.
</Info>
